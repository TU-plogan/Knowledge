APERTURE, the opening of any thing, or a hole or cleft in any continuous object.
[7:3:282]

Aperture, in Geometry, the space between two right lines which meet in a point and form an angle.

Aperture, in Optics, a round hole in a turned bit of , wood or plate of tin, placed within the side or a telescope or microscope, near to the object-glass, by means of which more rays are admitted, and a more distinct appearance of the object is obtained.
