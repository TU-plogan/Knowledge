WATLINGTON, an irregularly built market-town of the county of Oxford, in the hundred of Pirton, forty-three miles from London. It stands in a hilly district, called the Chiltern Hills, has an endowed school, and a market on Saturday, with two annual fairs. The population amounted in 1821 to 1479, and in 1831 to 1833.
