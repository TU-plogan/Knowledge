BONNET, in a general sense, denotes a cover for the head, in common use before the introduction of hats.

Bonnet, in Fortification, a small work consisting of two faces, having only a parapet with two rows of palisades, at about ten or twelve feet distance. It is generally raised before the salient angle of the counterscarp, and has a communication with the covered way, by a trench cut through the glacis, and palisades on each side.

Bonnet a Prctre, or Priest's Bonnet, in Fortification, is an out-work, having at the head three salient and two rentrant angles. Bonnet, in sea-language, denotes an addition to a sail.
