CAMISARDS, a name given by the French to the Calvinists of the Cevennes, who formed a league, and took up arms in their own defence, in 1688.

CΛMISQ, a city of the intendancy of Seragosa, in the island of Sicily. It is situated at the foot of the mountain Modia, but healthy, and contains 10,000 inhabitants.

CΛMLETINE, a slight stuff, made of hair and coarse silk, in the manner of camblet.
