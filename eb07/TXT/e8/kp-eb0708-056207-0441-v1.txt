ELAPHEBOLIUM, in Grecian antiquity, the ninth month of the Athenian year, answering to the latter part of February and the beginning of March. It consisted of thirty days, and took its name from the festival Elaphe-l>olia, observed in this month in honour of Diana the huntress, as mentioned above.
