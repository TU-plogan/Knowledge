ADMINISTRATOR, in English Law, he to whom the ordinary commits the administration of the goods of a person deeeased, in default of an executor. If the administrator die, his executors are not administrators; but the court is to grant a new administration. The origin of ad-, ministrators is derived from the eivil law. Their establishment in England is owing to a statute made in the 31st year of Edward III. Till then, no offiee of this kind was known beside that of exeeutor; in ease of a want of whieh, the ordinary had the disposal of goods of persons intestate, &e.
