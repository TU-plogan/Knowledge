AEQUIMELIUM, in Antiquity, a plaee in Rome where stood the house of Spurius Melius, who, by largesses corrupting the people, affeeted the supreme power. Refusing to appear before the dictator Cineinnatus, he was slain by Servilius Ahala, master of the horse; his house was razed to the ground, and the spot on whieh it stood was ealled Area Aequimelii. (Livy.)
