MORPHEUS, in fabulous history, the god of sleep, or, according to others, one of the ministers of Somnus. He caused drowsiness, and represented the forms of dreams.

Ovid styles him the kindest of the deities; and he is usually described in a recumbent posture, crowned with poppies.
