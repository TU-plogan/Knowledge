TRANSPOSIT ΙΟΝ, in Grammar, a disturbing or dislocating the words of a discourse, or a changing their natural order of construction, to please the ear, by rendering the contexture more smooth, easy, and harmonious.

Transposition, in Music, the removal of a piece of music, either in writing or in performing it, into a key higher or lower than the original one. For the principle of this, see Music, p. 610.
