PLUSH, in commerce, a kind of stuff having a sort of velvet knap or shag on one side, composed regularly of a woof of a single woollen thread and a double warp; the one wool, of two threads twisted, the other goat’s or camel’s hair; though there are some plushes entirely of worsted, and others composed wholly of hair.
