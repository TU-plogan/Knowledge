PROPOSITION, in Logic, part of an argument, in which some quality, either negative or positive, is attributed to a subject.

Proposition, in Mathematics, is either some truth advanced and shown to be such by demonstration, or some [7:18:664] operation proposed and its solution explained. If the proposition be deduced from several theoretical definitions compared together, it is called a theorem ; if from a praxis, or series of operations, it is called & problem.
