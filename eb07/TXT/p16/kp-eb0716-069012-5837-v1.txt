PACUVIUS, Marcus, of Brundusium, in Calabria, a tragic poet of high reputation, who flourished about the year of Rome 600. He was nephew of Ennius; published several theatrical pieces, of which only some fragments remain; and died at Tarentum at above ninety years of age.
