BOURDELOT, John, a learned French critic, who lived at the close of the sixteenth and beginning of the seventeenth centuries. He distinguished himself by annotations on Lucian, Petronius, and Heliodorus; a Universal History; Commentaries on Juvenal; a Treatise on the Etymology of French words; and several other works which were never published.
