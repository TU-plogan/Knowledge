BRAURONI A, in Grecian Antiquity, a festival in honour of Diana, surnamed Brauronia, from its having been observed at Brauron, an Athenian borough. This festival was celebrated once in five years, under the management of ten men, ealled in Greek kοοπο∣ο∣. The vietim offered in saerifiee was a goat, and it was eustomary for certain men to sing portions of Homer’s Iliad. The most remarkable persons at this solemnity were young virgins, habited in yellow gowns, and conseeratcd to Diana. It was unlawful for any of them tobe above ten or under five years of age.
