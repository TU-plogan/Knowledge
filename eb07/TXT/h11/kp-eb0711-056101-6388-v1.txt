HOPLITES, Hoplitae (formed from ⅛λo∣∕, armour}, in Antiquity, were such of the candidates at the Olympic and other sacred games as ran races in armour. One of the finest pieces of Parrhasius was a painting representing two hoplites; the one running, and seeming to sweat large drops, and the other laying down his arms, exhausted and out of breath.
