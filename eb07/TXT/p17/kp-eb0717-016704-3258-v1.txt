PECS, a city of the Austrian kingdom of Hungary, in the province of the Hither Danube, and circle of Baranyer. It is situated on a river of the same name, upon whose banks it extends in a single street more than six miles in length. It is the sec of a bishop, the seat of a Catholic seminary, and contains conventsand hospitals, with 2160 houses, and 9640 inhabitants, who produce vinegar, tobacco, starch, and some good wine. It has also much trade, arising from several great fairs for cattle and leather, which are held there. Long. 18. 10. E. Lat. 46. 5. 13. N.
