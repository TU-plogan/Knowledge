GHEP, or Gheep, a district of Hindustan, in the province of Lahore, situated principally between the thirty-second and thirty-third degrees of north latitude. It is bounded on the north-west by Sohaan, and on the southeast by Jhylum rivers. It produces a considerable quantity of fossil salt, great part of which is exported to the more eastern and southern provinces of Hindustan. Part of it belongs to the SikkÎ², and the remainder to the Afghans. The principal towns are Pirhala, Muckealab, and Varsha.
