KORNA, a flourishing little village of Irak Arabi. It is situated at the confluence of the Euphrates and the Tigris, and carries on some trade with Bassora. It was recommended by Sir J. Malcolm as a fort which might [7:12:760] be occupied and fortified, and from which the predatory Arabs might be kept in check.
